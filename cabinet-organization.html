Introductory information

The NGO Healthcare Platform is an international non-governmental membership organization registered in Tallinn, Estonia. We are an association of non-governmental organizations and individuals working in the field of HIV infection and other sexual health issues of gay men, other MSM and trans people in Eastern Europe and Central Asia (EECA).

Since 2017, NGO Healthcare Platform has been implementing the “Right to Health” program, which aims to increase the number of MSM and trans people who have constant access to the continuum of HIV services in EECA.

The Eurasian Men's Health Coalition announces a competition for the selection of a consultant for mapping available services for trans people in organizations and medical institutions in the Kyrgyz Republic under the Right to Health program with the support of the Global Fund.
Introductory information

The NGO Healthcare Platform is an international non-governmental membership organization registered in Tallinn, Estonia. We are an association of non-governmental organizations and individuals working in the field of HIV infection and other sexual health issues of gay men, other MSM and trans people in Eastern Europe and Central Asia (EECA).

Since 2017, NGO Healthcare Platform has been implementing the “Right to Health” program, which aims to increase the number of MSM and trans people who have constant access to the continuum of HIV services in EECA.

The main activities of the program are implemented at the regional level by the NGO Healthcare Platform secretariat, and at the country level - by the sub-recipients of the program in five countries: Armenia, Belarus, Georgia, Kyrgyzstan and Macedonia. In Kyrgyzstan, NGO Healthcare Platform supports the work of the Kyrgyz Indigo Public Association in the framework of the Right to Health program.

In the framework of the provision of technical assistance to the NGO “Kyrgyz Indigo”, NGO Healthcare Platform announces a tender for the selection of a consultant for conducting mapping of affordable services for trans people in organizations and medical institutions in the Kyrgyz Republic.

Research

Objective:
To determine whether the services provided to trans people by public organizations and government bodies in the context of HIV prevention and health in the Kyrgyz Republic are attractive.

Objectives of the study:
Review services for trans people today;
Create a tool for mapping available services for trans people;
Determine whether the services provided meet the needs of trans people;
Develop targeted recommendations to medical organizations, government institutions, NGOs, donors working in the fields of HIV and providing services for transgender people to improve access to and quality of services for trans people, as well as to involve trans people in decision-making processes.
